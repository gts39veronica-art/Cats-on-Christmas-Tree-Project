Cats and Christmas Trees

Cats can be very annoying and chaotic during Christmas. Especially when there is a Christmas tree nearby. Cats love Christmas trees because they trigger natural instincts: the tree is a new, exciting climbing structure, a great lookout spot, a fun scratching post, and its dangling, shiny decorations mimic toys, providing the ultimate indoor jungle gym for hunting, hiding, and playing, appealing to their predatory and security-seeking nature. 

Ways to Deal with Cats on Christmas Trees

To deal with cats and Christmas trees, use deterrents like citrus scents or aluminum foil  on the base, secure the tree to the wall to prevent tipping, place fragile ornaments higher up, and remove "launch pads" like nearby furniture. You can also create a fun alternative for your cat with their own tree or catnip toys nearby and use barriers like pet gates for added protection.
